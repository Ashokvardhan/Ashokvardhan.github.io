      • Research

      I work broadly in foundations of data science in topics including machine learning, information theory, coding theory, and optmization. In particular, I am interested in themes that require a creative amalgamation of these diverse fields towards a multifaceted perspective of data science - with its ever growing ubiquity and prominence in both research and daily life alike (e.g. ChatGPT), there is a commensurate need for understanding its success and pitfalls. My research aims to address this using a first-principles approach to solve theoretical, algorithmic, and practical problems at the heart of this arena. The following are some representative works:

      • Inventing error-correcting codes (Machine learning Information theory): KO codes, Reed-Muller subcodes, CRISP, and TinyTurbo.

      • Wireless distributed optimization and federated learning (Machine learning Information theory): LASER.

      • Learning optimal transport maps (Optimization & Probability Machine learning): OT maps.

      • Mixture-of-Experts (Optimization & Statistics Machine learning): MoE and Gated neural networks.

      • Information-theoretic LLMs (Information theory Machine learning): Stay tuned!
