About

Hi there! I am a postdoctoral researcher at EPFL working with Michael Gastpar, Martin Jaggi, and Caglar Gulcehre. My primary research interests are in interpretability and explainability of LLMs. In particular, I am interested in explaining the fundamental sequential modeling capabilities of various language models such as Transformers and SSMs, and explaining the key differences between these classes of architectures. Some recent works along this theme are: Fundamental limits of prompt compression, Attention with Markov, Local to Global, and Constant depth suffices.

Earlier, I got my PhD in Electrical and Computer Engineering from UIUC working with Pramod Viswanath and Sewoong Oh. During my PhD, I have also had the pleasure of working with Jason Lee and Sreeram Kannan (Founder and CEO of EigenLayer).

Prior to that, I graduated from IIT Bombay with a B. Tech. (Honors) in Electrical Engineering and Minors in Mathematics working with Vivek Borkar.
