About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at EPFL, working with Michael Gastpar, Jason Lee, and Martin Jaggi. My research focuses on building principled and foundational AI frameworks that enable reliable and trustworthy AI systems. In particular, my work bridges theoretical insights and practical systems across two key research thrusts: (1) Thrust 1 — Algorithmic Frameworks for AI via Information-Theoretic Principles, and (2) Thrust 2 — Theoretical Foundations of AI via Structured Data. I have made fundamental contributions to these areas, with my research featured in top-tier machine learning conferences such as ICML and NeurIPS. A selection of key publications reflecting this theme includes: Fundamental limits of prompt compression, Attention with Markov, Local to Global, and Constant depth suffices.

Before joining EPFL, I got my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from UIUC, where I worked with Pramod Viswanath and Sewoong Oh. During my PhD, I had the pleasure of collaborating with and Sreeram Kannan, Founder and CEO of EigenLayer.

Prior to that, I graduated from IIT Bombay with a B. Tech. (Honors) in Electrical Engineering and Minors in Mathematics, where I worked with Vivek Borkar.
