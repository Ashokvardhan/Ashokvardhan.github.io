Me and Research Vision

I am a postdoctoral researcher at EPFL, working with Michael Gastpar, Jason Lee, and Martin Jaggi. My research establishes AI Foundations to build reliable and interpretable AI systems. To this end, my work has delivered impactful practical advances and key theoretical insights across two main research thrusts: (1) Thrust 1 (Reliability) — Algorithmic Frameworks for AI via Information-Theoretic Principles, and (2) Thrust 2 (Interpretability) — Theoretical Foundations of AI via Structured Data. My fundamental contributions across these areas have appeared in top-tier machine learning venues such as NeurIPS, ICLR, and ICML, and have been been recognized with an ICLR Spotlight Award, a Best Paper Award from ACM MobiHoc, the Joan and Lalit Bahl Fellowship (twice), the Sundaram Seshu International Student Fellowship, and a Qualcomm Innovation Fellowship for two mentored students.

I have delivered invited talks at leading institutions, including Stanford, Berkeley, and Microsoft Research, as well as a tutorial at NeurIPS and an upcoming invited article in IEEE BITS Magazine. A selection of key publications reflecting this theme includes: Fundamental limits of prompt compression, Attention with Markov, Local to Global, and Constant depth suffices.

Before joining EPFL, I got my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from UIUC, where I worked with Pramod Viswanath and Sewoong Oh. During my PhD, I had the pleasure of collaborating with and Sreeram Kannan, Founder and CEO of EigenLayer.

Prior to that, I graduated from IIT Bombay with a B. Tech. (Honors) in Electrical Engineering and Minors in Mathematics, where I worked with Vivek Borkar.
